Enterprise AI-native project cases

Selected case formats for showing how I frame enterprise problems, design AI-native operating models, and turn strategic ambition into shipped systems.

Case 01

AI-native knowledge operations for a complex service organization

Mapped fragmented knowledge workflows, designed retrieval and review loops, and defined a path from expert-dependent operations to reusable institutional memory.

  • Problem: tacit knowledge trapped across teams and tools
  • Approach: RAG workflow, source governance, human review checkpoints
  • Outcome: faster research cycles and clearer accountability for generated answers
Case 02

Agent-assisted operating model for sales and customer success

Translated scattered customer signals into a structured AI workflow for account preparation, follow-up generation, and escalation detection.

  • Problem: high-value context lost between meetings, CRM notes, and support logs
  • Approach: workflow agent, confidence thresholds, role-based handoff design
  • Outcome: better preparation quality and more consistent customer touchpoints
Case 03

Executive AI transformation roadmap and pilot portfolio

Helped prioritize where AI should change operating leverage first, separating attractive demos from initiatives with clear adoption and measurement paths.

  • Problem: too many AI ideas, too little transformation discipline
  • Approach: opportunity scoring, risk map, pilot portfolio design
  • Outcome: clearer sequencing for leadership, product, and implementation teams

Method

How I approach AI-native transformation

I care less about isolated AI features and more about whether the organization can repeatedly use AI to make better decisions, move faster, and preserve trust.

01

Diagnose the workflow

Start with decisions, handoffs, latency, risk, and ownership before choosing models or tools.

02

Design the operating loop

Define where AI acts, where humans review, how exceptions route, and what evidence is logged.

03

Measure adoption and trust

Track usage quality, confidence, error recovery, and business leverage instead of demo impressiveness.
